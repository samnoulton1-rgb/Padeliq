OUR STORY

Built from a love of padel—and the questions that follow every match

PadelIQ began with a simple frustration: after leaving court, it was hard to understand what had really shaped the match. Memory captures the winners, but not the positioning, recovery and movement patterns behind every point.

We are building the tool we wanted as players: an accessible coach that turns match footage into clear, useful feedback. The goal is to help every player see their game more objectively, practise with purpose and enjoy the journey of improving.

      THE COMPLETE PLAYER

      Five foundations, one connected game

      Good padel is less about isolated winners and more about repeatable decisions, coordinated movement and earning the right ball to attack.

      LOBMOVEPIVOTPAIRRESETYOU

      ANIMATED COACHING

      See positioning and movement in action

      Original PadelIQ animations demonstrate repeatable movement patterns. Pause any clip and rehearse the sequence at your own pace.

      LOOPING ANIMATION

      Advance together after a successful lob

      Move as a pair, stop before the opponent contacts, then split step. Do not leave one player stranded at the back.

      LOOPING ANIMATION

      Recover immediately after your shot

      After defending the corner, return toward your useful base and your partner’s line before watching the outcome.

      LOOPING ANIMATION

      Shift together and protect the middle

      Follow the ball laterally while keeping a recoverable gap. The player opposite the ball protects the middle.

      RETURN DECISION TRAINER

      Where should you return the ball?

      Choose where the incoming ball reaches you. The court highlights the higher-margin target that generally reduces unforced-error risk.

      SAFER TARGET

      Cross-court, deep through the middle

      Use the longer diagonal and clear the net through its lower centre. Aim with margin rather than close to the glass.

      Tactical guidance is situational—not a guarantee. Adjust for ball height, speed, score and opponent position.

      FOUNDATION 01

      Play the lob with purpose

      Use height and depth to move opponents off the net, buy recovery time and advance with your partner.

      • Contact in front with an open face
      • Aim beyond the service line
      • Move forward only when the lob succeeds
      TRAINING DRILLTen cross-court lobs: score one point for depth, one for allowing both players to advance.
      ◖◗2
      FOUNDATION 02

      Move efficiently

      Use small adjustment steps near contact and decisive first steps when recovering or changing zones.

      • Split step as the opponent contacts
      • Keep your centre of gravity controlled
      • Recover immediately after your shot
      TRAINING DRILLShadow six balls with no racket; return to base after every movement.
      ↻3
      FOUNDATION 03

      Pivot before you run

      For balls behind you, turn the hips and shoulders first so you can track the ball without losing balance.

      • Open the outside foot first
      • Keep the ball visible over your shoulder
      • Avoid backpedalling under pressure
      TRAINING DRILLAlternate left and right pivots on a coach signal, then recover to the same base marker.
      4
      FOUNDATION 04

      Move as a pair

      Protect the middle and remove easy gaps by advancing, retreating and shifting laterally on the same line.

      • Keep a recoverable distance from your partner
      • Follow the ball together
      • Communicate early and simply
      TRAINING DRILLTie an imaginary four-metre rope between partners and maintain it through a cooperative rally.
      ↙↘5
      FOUNDATION 05

      Reset to a useful base

      The shot is not finished at contact. Recover to the position that best protects the next likely ball.

      • Finish balanced enough to move again
      • Recover toward your partner line
      • Use the heatmap to find repeated gaps
      TRAINING DRILLPlace a base marker and touch it between every two controlled shots.

      POSITION LIKE A PRO

      A repeatable positioning system

      Use these five checkpoints before, during and immediately after every shot.

      1. 1
        Start from a useful base

        Stay connected to your partner, roughly level with them and close enough to protect the middle.

      2. 2
        Read before moving

        Use opponent preparation and ball height to move early instead of reacting after contact.

      3. 3
        Advance and retreat together

        Keep the partnership on the same horizontal line so opponents cannot attack the gap.

      4. 4
        Recover after every action

        Your movement is not finished at contact; return to the best base for the next likely ball.

      5. 5
        Review the heatmap

        Look for isolated hotspots, empty channels and excessive time trapped in transition.
